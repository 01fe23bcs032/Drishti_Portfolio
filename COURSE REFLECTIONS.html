Course Learning Reflections

1. Iteration, Recursion, and Backtracking

  • Iteration: Repeatedly performing a task, e.g., spiral arrangements in flowers.
  • Recursion: Breaking a problem into smaller sub-problems, e.g., cell division.
  • Backtracking: Exploring possibilities and reverting when necessary, e.g., Sudoku.

2. Space and Time Efficiency

  • Space Efficiency: Memory required by an algorithm during execution.
  • Time Efficiency: Time taken to complete a task. Helps evaluate an algorithm's practicality.

3. Design Principles

Using the right principle simplifies problems:

  • Pruning: Ideal for N-Queens problem.
  • Bit Manipulation: Used in Fenwick Trees.
